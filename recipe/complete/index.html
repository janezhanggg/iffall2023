HOW TO MAKE

MANGO SAGO

scroll for recipe

Mango sago is a tropical Asian dessert originating from Hong Kong that combines mangoes, sago pearls, and coconut milk to make the perfect summer dessert!

Ingredients

  • 3 mangoes
  • 200 mL coconut milk (about half a can)
  • 4 Tbsp condensed milk or sweetener of choice
  • 1/8 cup salt
  • 1/2 cup sago pearls

Cook Time: 25 min

Chill Time (optional): 1-2 hours

Yield: 5 servings

Instructions

1.

Bring 5 cups of water to a boil. Once boiling, throw in the uncooked sago pearls and turn down the heat to a medium-low. Let the pearls cook for around 15 minutes (stirring constantly to avoid sticking/burning), or until the pearls are translucent and no longer white.

2.

Once cooked, drain the boiling liquid and immediately rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

3.

In a blender, combine together two diced mangoes, coconut milk, condensed milk, and salt. Blend until smooth. You may choose to chill this mixture for at least 1-2 hours if you prefer the dessert to be cold.

4.

Gently fold the mango-coconut mixture with the cooked sago pearls.

5.

Layer with diced mangoes and serve!

NOTE: Mango sago can be kept refrigerated; however, the consistency of the sago pearls will reduce in quality after being refrigerated overnight and will become firm and doughy. However, the mango-coconut mixture can be made ahead of time and stored for several days. Therefore, it is best served on the same day that the sago pearls are cooked and added.
